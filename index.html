Western Sichuan Travel Diary

Introduction

Welcome to my Sichuan (Chuanxi) Travel Diary where I embark on a journey in pursuit of beauty and soul-searching. Here, you'll accompany me throughout Garzê's Seda, where we'll embrace the tranquility of Tagong Grassland, step into the Tibetan village Jiaju in Danba County, and together long for that mysterious and beautiful land.

Four days in western Sichuan

The four-day journey through Sichuan West is an unforgettable exploration that covers areas such as Chengdu, Kangding, Daocheng, and Yading. Over these four days, we will immerse ourselves in rich Tibetan culture, appreciate the majestic beauty of nature, enjoy local cuisine, and experience a truly unique journey. Below is a detailed itinerary for the four days, hopefully providing a useful reference for your trip:

Day 1: Chengdu - Tianquan (Sightseeing) - Kangding

In the morning, you can leave Chengdu and head to Tianquan via Shimian. You can visit Tianquan County with the beautiful ancient town of Moxi and Moon Bay Scenic Spot. The old streets and ancient bridges are the highlights here. In the evening, arrive in Kangding for accommodation.

Day 2: Kangding - Daocheng

Departing from Kangding, you can view the beautiful scenery of Mugecuo, Cuoka Lake, and other places along the way. Then drive to Daocheng, and stay overnight in Daocheng.

Day 3: Daocheng - Yading

In Yading, you can see snowy mountains, grasslands, and lakes; the scenery is very beautiful. If your physical condition allows, you can also see the Five-Color Sea and Milk Sea. Yading is often regarded as "the last pure land on the blue planet." Stay overnight in Yading.

Day 4: Daocheng - Chengdu

On the last day, we head back from Daocheng to Chengdu. Take a break at Xindu Bridge to appreciate its natural beauty along the way. Once we arrive back in Chengdu, we'll wrap up the day with a delicious Sichuan hotpot dinner, a perfect way to end your journey in Sichuan.

Precautions / travelers need to take note

Adapt to altitude sickness

Due to higher altitude of the West Sichuan plateau, newcomers may suffer altitude sickness - a normal physiological reaction. It's suggested to stay a day or two in cities with lower altitudes before gradually moving to higher grounds, allowing your body to adapt slowly.

Guard against sunburn and frostbite

The UV radiation in West Sichuan is relatively strong, while the daylight duration is long and the temperature difference is huge. Make sure to use sunscreen during the day and dress warmly at night.

Respect local customs and faith

The local residents in West Sichuan are extremely devoted to their religious beliefs, and there are many customs to observe. While visiting attractions or observing religious activities, we must respect local customs and traditions and try not to disrupt the locals in their daily lives and religious practices.
